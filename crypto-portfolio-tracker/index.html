Best Crypto Portfolio Tracker (2026 Guide)

Choosing the best crypto portfolio tracker depends on one simple question: do you want convenience, deep reporting, or full control? This guide compares the three major approaches: mobile apps, exchange dashboards, and spreadsheet-based tracking systems.

What is the best crypto portfolio tracker?

  • Best for beginners: mobile apps like Delta.
  • Best for tax-heavy reporting: Koinly or CoinTracker.
  • Best for ownership & long-term clarity: structured spreadsheet systems.

1. Mobile App Portfolio Trackers

Apps like Delta or CoinStats focus on ease of use. You connect exchanges, sync balances, and see a clean dashboard.

  • Fast onboarding
  • Good mobile UX
  • Notifications & alerts
  • Often subscription-based

The trade-off is control. Calculations happen behind the scenes. You rely on their sync engine and pricing logic.

2. Exchange Dashboards

If you keep assets on one exchange, their built-in dashboard may be enough.

  • Shows balances held on that platform
  • Limited cross-exchange visibility
  • Often no real cost basis tracking

The limitation is obvious: once assets move across wallets or exchanges, clarity disappears.

3. Spreadsheet-Based Crypto Trackers

A structured spreadsheet system is different. It separates:

  • TRADES (input)
  • HOLDINGS (calculation)
  • STAKING (separate balance)
  • PRICES (auto + fallback)
  • DASHBOARD (output)

This approach gives full transparency. No black-box formulas. No subscription. No platform dependency.
