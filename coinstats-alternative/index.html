What CoinStats does well (and where it falls short)

CoinStats is popular because it’s convenient: it can connect to exchanges/wallets and show a portfolio dashboard fast. The downside is that convenience usually comes with tradeoffs: subscriptions, limited customization, and a platform-controlled workflow.

  • Good for: quick portfolio view, integrations, basic alerts.
  • Not ideal for: long-term auditability, custom rules, and avoiding monthly fees.

Two types of “CoinStats alternatives” (don’t mix them)

1) Investing apps (buy & invest)

These are not portfolio trackers first — they’re execution platforms. Use them if your primary goal is buying, selling, staking, recurring buys, and investing features.

  • Coinbase — simplest onboarding, beginner-friendly UX.
  • Kraken — security-first reputation, stable platform.
  • Binance — most features and asset coverage, low fees.

2) Portfolio trackers (analytics)

These focus on performance reporting, allocation, and tracking — not executing trades. They can be great, but many still rely on subscriptions and closed dashboards.

  • Delta — strong mobile tracking UI and alerts.
  • Koinly / CoinTracker — heavy on reporting/import workflows.
  • CoinGecko / CoinMarketCap portfolios — good free monitoring, limited depth.

Best practice: keep execution (exchange app) separate from analytics (tracker). You’ll get fewer sync problems and better clarity.

FAQ

Is CoinStats worth it?

CoinStats can be worth it if you rely heavily on integrations and don’t mind subscriptions. If you want ownership and auditability, a Sheets-based workflow is often a better long-term solution.

What is the best free CoinStats alternative?

CoinGecko and CoinMarketCap offer basic portfolio tracking for free. They’re good for monitoring, but they don’t usually provide a clean cost basis workflow or a protected system designed for long-term clarity.

Which alternative lets me buy crypto?

If you want to buy/invest directly, use an exchange app like Coinbase, Kraken, or Binance. Then use a tracker (like Crypto Planner CORE) for analytics and reporting.

Do I need API keys to track my portfolio?

No. API keys are optional and add complexity. A tracker that works without APIs reduces risk and keeps you in control.
