Why people look for a CoinStats alternative

CoinStats is popular because it’s convenient: connect exchanges/wallets, see a dashboard, track performance. But many users switch when one of these becomes a dealbreaker:

  • Subscriptions — paying monthly for features you only need for portfolio tracking.
  • Sync issues — mismatched balances, missing staking, delayed updates.
  • Limited control — you can’t fully customize logic, categories, cost basis rules.
  • Data ownership — your tracking depends on a platform and its pricing/limits.
If you want the short answer

If your goal is buying & investing, use an investing app (exchange). If your goal is clean portfolio tracking, use a dedicated tracker — or better: a tracker you own.

Two types of CoinStats alternatives

1) Investing apps (buy & invest)

Best when you want: buy/sell, recurring buys, staking, earn products, fast execution.

  • Mobile-first UX
  • Built for trading & investing
  • Portfolio view is usually “good enough” — not perfect

2) Portfolio trackers (analytics)

Best when you want: cost basis, PnL accuracy, allocation, clean reporting.

  • Better performance analytics
  • Often requires syncing/import
  • Still frequently subscription-based

CoinStats alternatives that let you buy & invest crypto (mobile apps)

If Google shows you “apps to invest” in the top results — that’s a strong signal: many people actually want execution, not just tracking.

Best for simplicity: Coinbase

Clean onboarding, beginner-friendly, strong compliance. Best when you want the simplest investing UX.

Best for features: Binance

Huge asset coverage, lots of investing products, advanced trading tools. Best when you want options and low fees.

Best for security-first investing: Kraken

Strong reputation, stable platform. Best when you want a conservative, robust exchange experience.

Important

Investing apps are great for buying and staking — but they are not designed to be your long-term analytics engine. If you want accurate portfolio clarity, you still need a dedicated tracker.

CoinStats alternatives for portfolio tracking (analytics)

These options focus more on tracking and reporting than on execution:

Delta

Strong mobile tracker experience, alerts, good UI. Often still pushes you toward premium features.

Koinly / CoinTracker

Great for imports and reporting workflows. Often used for tax contexts; can be heavy if you just want clarity.

CoinGecko / CoinMarketCap portfolios

Good free monitoring. Limited depth for cost basis and long-term portfolio control.

The “ownership” option: a tracker you own (Google Sheets)

If you want maximum control, privacy, and no subscriptions, a structured portfolio tracker in Google Sheets can outperform apps — because you control logic, categories, and data.

FAQ

Is CoinStats worth it?

It can be worth it if you rely on automatic syncing across many exchanges and don’t mind subscriptions. If you want long-term clarity with ownership and customization, a spreadsheet-based tracker is often the smarter move.

What is the best free CoinStats alternative?

CoinGecko and CoinMarketCap offer basic portfolio tracking for free. They’re good for monitoring, but they typically don’t provide a clean cost-basis workflow or structured long-term portfolio system.

Which CoinStats alternative lets me buy crypto?

If you want to buy/invest, you’re looking for an exchange app (e.g., Coinbase, Binance, Kraken). Those are execution tools. For analytics and portfolio clarity, use a dedicated tracker alongside them.

Do I need API keys to track my portfolio?

No. API keys are optional. A tracker that works without API keys reduces risk and gives you full control. Crypto Planner CORE is designed to be usable without any exchange API connection.
