If you're looking for a Google Sheets crypto tracker, you're probably tired of subscription-based apps, black-box calculations, and dashboards you can't fully control. The good news? A properly structured spreadsheet can outperform many paid crypto tracking tools — without monthly fees.

Why Use Google Sheets to Track Crypto?

Most crypto tracking apps are built for convenience. Google Sheets is built for control. When structured correctly, it allows you to:

  • Track spot holdings
  • Track staking separately
  • Calculate real cost basis
  • Monitor unrealized PnL
  • Control price sources
  • Avoid subscriptions
  • Own your portfolio data completely

What Most Google Sheets Crypto Templates Get Wrong

  • They only track current price, not weighted cost basis.
  • They break when you add new trades.
  • They mix manual and automatic prices without logic.
  • They ignore staking balances.
  • They allow editing of calculated cells.

A serious tracker needs structure, not just formulas.

The 5-Layer Structure of a Proper Crypto Tracker

1) TRADES (Input Layer)

Clean transaction log: date, asset, side, quantity, price, fees. This is your source of truth.

2) HOLDINGS (Calculation Layer)

Aggregates trades into totals per asset and calculates weighted average cost, value and unrealized PnL.

3) STAKING (Separate & Summed)

Track staking independently and then add it to totals — otherwise performance metrics get distorted.

4) PRICES (Auto + Manual Fallback)

Automatic prices first, manual override only when an API fails or a token is too niche.

5) DASHBOARD (Output Only)

Output-only: total value, PnL, allocation, top holdings — no raw edits.
